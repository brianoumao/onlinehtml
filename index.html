Breakout Success: He began his career in 2013 and gained regional acclaim with mixtapes. His 2016 collaboration EP with producer Metro Boomin, "Savage Mode," propelled him to national fame, landing at #23 on the Billboard 200 and featuring hit singles like "X" and "No Heart." Top of the Charts: With two #1 albums ("Savage Mode 2" in 2020 and "Her Loss" with Drake in 2022), numerous solo and collaborative songs, and platinum certifications, 21 Savage has established himself as a leading figure in contemporary hip-hop. Grammy Recognition: His raw and introspective lyrics, often reflecting his turbulent past, earned him two Grammy nominations in 2020, winning one for Best Rap Song with "A Lot.
